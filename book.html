About the Work

Where Classical Wisdom
Meets Digital Ambition

The original Arthashastra of Kautilya — written circa 300 BCE — was the most comprehensive science of statecraft ever produced. It understood, with prescient clarity, that the sovereign who comprehends the information environment of his era holds the decisive advantage.

Arthashastra 2.0 applies this ancient method to the most consequential challenge facing Bangladesh today: artificial intelligence. Through thirty original Sutras, ten chapters of rigorous scholarship, and more than forty peer-reviewed citations, it charts a course from digital dependency to sovereign trust.

For the practitioner, the policymaker, and the informed citizen — in the language of Kautilya, the grammar of the present.

The Author

Practitioner.
Scholar. Strategist.

Md Taufiqul Islam is a Joint Secretary at the Cabinet Division of the Government of Bangladesh. A graduate of Monash University's Master of Environment and Sustainability programme, his work bridges the worlds of public administration, digital policy, and strategic governance.

"The AI is a telescope that sees a compressed version of the entire internet. It can see far, but it has no eyes. We are the eye."

His research draws on Kautilya's Arthashastra, contemporary political economy, and the lived reality of governing a nation of 170 million people at the frontier of the AI transition. This book is the culmination of that inquiry.
