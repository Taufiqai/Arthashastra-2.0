About the Work

Where Classical Wisdom
Meets Digital Ambition

The original Arthashastra of Kautilya — written circa 300 BCE — was the most comprehensive science of statecraft ever produced. It understood that the sovereign who comprehends the information environment of his era holds the decisive advantage.

Arthashastra 2.0 applies this ancient method to the most consequential challenge facing Bangladesh today: artificial intelligence. Through thirty original sutras, ten chapters of rigorous scholarship, and more than forty peer-reviewed citations, it charts a course from digital dependency to sovereign trust.

The Author

Practitioner.
Scholar. Strategist.

Md Taufiqul Islam is a Joint Secretary at the Cabinet Division of the Government of Bangladesh and a graduate of Monash University. His work bridges public administration, digital policy, and strategic governance.

"The AI is a telescope that sees a compressed version of the entire internet. It can see far, but it has no eyes. We are the eye."

This book is the culmination of an inquiry drawing on Kautilya's Arthashastra, contemporary political economy, and the lived reality of governing a nation of 170 million people at the frontier of the AI transition.

"The delta does not resist the flood. It absorbs it, works with it, and emerges — transformed but vital — on the other side."
